What is Affirmative Action?

Affirmative action is a policy designed to increase opportunities for historically disadvantaged groups in areas like education and employment.

How does Affirmative Action help?

In schools

Affirmative action in education fosters diversity, addresses historical injustices, and ensures equal opportunities, enriching learning environments and promoting social mobility. By supporting underrepresented groups, it prepares students for a diverse workforce and reflects a commitment to equality.

In employment

Affirmative action in employment fosters diversity and reduces discrimination by ensuring the inclusion of underrepresented groups. It boosts company reputation and promotes employee satisfaction, preparing businesses for the global market. This drives economic growth and benefit both employees and employers
